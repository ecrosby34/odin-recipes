Description

Beef Tapsilog is a popular Filipino dish that combines three main components: beef (tapa), garlic-fried rice (sinangag), and fried egg (itlog). The name "Tapsilog" is a portmanteau of the three main elements. Here's a breakdown of each component:

  1. Beef Tapa (Tapa): Thinly sliced beef marinated in a mixture of soy sauce, calamansi (Filipino lime), garlic, sugar, and sometimes vinegar. The beef is typically cured or marinated to achieve a flavorful and slightly sweet taste.

  2. Garlic-Fried Rice (Sinangag): Cooked rice stir-fried with minced garlic until golden brown. This adds a fragrant and savory element to the dish.

  3. Fried Egg (Itlog): A sunny-side-up or over-easy fried egg is served on top of the beef and rice. The runny yolk adds richness to the overall flavor.

Tapsilog is often enjoyed as a hearty breakfast or brunch option, and it's a popular choice in many Filipino eateries known as "tapsihan" or "silog" places. The term "silog" is derived from the combination of "sinangag" (garlic-fried rice) and "itlog" (egg), which are common components in various Filipino breakfast dishes. The dish is beloved for its savory, sweet, and satisfying flavor profile.

Ingredients

Here is a simple recipe to make Beef Tapsilog at home:

  • 500 grams thinly sliced beef (sirloin or flank works well)
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 3 tablespoons vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon minced garlic
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 3 cups cooked rice (preferably day-old)
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 tablespoons cooking oil
  • Eggs (1 per serving)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Marinate the Beef:

    • In a bowl, combine soy sauce, vinegar, brown sugar, minced garlic, and black pepper to create the marinade.
    • Add the thinly sliced beef to the marinade, ensuring each slice is well-coated.
    • Cover the bowl and let it marinate for at least 30 minutes or refrigerate overnight for more flavor.

  2. Cook the Beef:

    • Heat a pan over medium-high heat and add a bit of oil.
    • Cook the marinated beef slices until they are browned and slightly crispy. Set aside.

  3. Prepare the Garlic Fried Rice (Sinangag):

    • In a separate pan, heat oil over medium heat.
    • Add minced garlic and saut√© until golden brown.
    • Add the cooked rice to the pan and stir-fry until well-coated with the garlic-infused oil.
    • Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  4. Fry the Eggs:

    • In the same pan or a different one, fry eggs to your preference. Traditionally, sunny-side-up with a runny yolk is common.

  5. Assemble the Tapsilog:

    • Plate the garlic fried rice, top it with the cooked beef slices.
    • Place a fried egg on the side or on top of the beef.

  6. Serve:

    • Garnish with chopped green onions or fresh tomatoes if desired.
    • Serve immediately and enjoy your homemade Beef Tapsilog!

Feel free to adjust the seasoning and ingredients according to your taste preferences. This recipe offers the traditional flavors of Tapsilog, but you can always customize it to suit your liking!
